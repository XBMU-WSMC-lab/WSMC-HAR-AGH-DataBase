Waving Motion Capture Protocol
1. Motion Name
Waving
2. Objective
This protocol is intended to standardize the waving behavior of participants during motion capture in order to ensure consistency, repeatability, and reliability of the collected data.
This motion is primarily used to record upper-limb kinematic characteristics and related physiological signals during controlled waving movement.
3. Applicable Subjects
This protocol is applicable to participants who are able to independently stand and safely perform repeated upper-limb waving movements.
Individuals with acute musculoskeletal injuries, severe pain in the shoulder, elbow, wrist, neck, or upper back, significant fatigue, balance impairment, neurological movement disorders, or any other condition unsuitable for repeated arm movement should not participate in this motion capture task.
4. Environment and Equipment Requirements
(1) The capture area shall be flat, stable, non-slip, unobstructed, and provide sufficient space for safe upper-limb movement.
(2) If wearable sensors or markers are used, they shall be positioned correctly, securely attached, and free from noticeable looseness or displacement.
(3) Participants shall wear appropriate clothing that does not restrict upper-limb movement or obscure markers.
(4) The participant shall stand in a clearly defined capture area with enough frontal and lateral clearance to complete the waving movement naturally.
(5) If necessary, a demonstration of the standard waving movement may be provided before formal capture.
5. Motion Definition
The participant performs waving in an upright standing posture with the upper arms slightly abducted. The forearms move back and forth in front of the chest toward the side of the head.
Slight wrist extension or flexion may occur naturally during the movement. Elbow flexion shall range approximately from 30 to 70 degrees, and shoulder flexion shall range approximately from 30 to 60 degrees. 
The motion shall remain natural, controlled, stable, and continuous throughout the capture period. Excessive trunk movement, exaggerated shoulder elevation, abrupt arm motion, or irregular waving rhythm should be avoided.
6. Motion Execution Requirements
(1) Starting Posture
   ●The participant stands at the designated starting position in a natural upright posture.
   ●The head remains stable, with the gaze directed forward.
   ●The shoulders remain relaxed without obvious elevation.
   ●The upper arms are slightly abducted at the sides of the body.
   ●The forearms are positioned comfortably in front of the chest in preparation for the waving movement.
   ●The feet are placed naturally in a stable standing position.
(2) Motion Procedure
   ●Upon instruction, the participant begins the waving movement in a controlled manner.
   ●The forearms move back and forth in front of the chest toward the side of the head.
   ●The upper arms remain slightly abducted throughout the movement.
   ●Slight wrist extension or flexion may occur naturally during waving.
   ●Elbow flexion shall remain within approximately 30–0 degrees.
   ●Shoulder flexion shall remain within approximately 30–0 degrees.
   ●The movement shall remain natural, stable, and continuous throughout the capture period.
   ●Excessive trunk motion, exaggerated shoulder elevation, abrupt movement, or compensatory body sway should be avoided.
(3) Quantitative Control Indicators
   ●Body posture: upright standing posture
   ●Upper-arm position: slightly abducted
   ●Forearm movement: back-and-forth motion in front of the chest toward the side of the head
   ●Wrist motion: slight extension or flexion may occur naturally
   ●Elbow flexion range: approximately 30–0 degrees
   ●Shoulder flexion range: approximately 30–0 degrees
   ●Movement quality: natural, controlled, stable, and continuous  
7. Capture Procedure
(1) Preparation Phase
   ●Explain the motion requirements and precautions to the participant.
   ●Check the operating status of all equipment and the placement of wearable sensors or markers.
   ●Inspect the capture area and surrounding environment for safety.
   ●Ensure that the participant completes the necessary warm-up, especially for the shoulders, elbows, wrists, and neck.  
(2) Preliminary Phase
   ●The participant first performs a short trial of waving at a comfortable pace.
   ●Adjust the motion until the required upper-arm position, forearm path, elbow flexion range, and shoulder flexion range are achieved.
   ●Begin formal capture only after the movement pattern is stable.
(3) Main Experiment Phase
   ●The researcher gives the start instruction.
   ●The participant performs waving in accordance with this protocol.
   ●During the capture, the researcher monitors whether the motion conforms to the required standard.
   ●If necessary, the researcher provides immediate reminder or safety supervision.
(4) Termination Phase
   ●At the end of the scheduled capture, the researcher gives the stop instruction.
   ●The participant returns to a stable natural standing posture.
   ●The researcher checks data quality and records any abnormal events or deviations.
8. Criteria for a Valid Trial
A waving trial may be considered valid if all of the following conditions are met:
   ●The participant maintains an upright standing posture throughout the task.
   ●The upper arms remain slightly abducted.
   ●The forearms move back and forth in front of the chest toward the side of the head.
   ●Any wrist extension or flexion remains slight and natural.
   ●Elbow flexion remains within approximately 30–0 degrees.
   ●Shoulder flexion remains within approximately 30–0 degrees.
   ●The movement is natural, controlled, continuous, and free of obvious instability or compensation.
9. Common Incorrect Patterns
The following conditions shall be regarded as motion deviations and may require re-capture:
   ●The participant does not maintain an upright standing posture.
   ●The upper arms are not slightly abducted or are positioned too close to the trunk.
   ●The forearms do not move in a clear back-and-forth pattern in front of the chest toward the side of the head.
   ●Wrist motion is exaggerated rather than slight and natural.
   ●Elbow flexion is clearly outside the approximate 30–0 degree range.
   ●Shoulder flexion is clearly outside the approximate 30–0 degree range.
   ●The participant uses excessive trunk movement, shoulder elevation, or body sway to complete the motion.
   ●The motion rhythm is irregular, abrupt, or poorly controlled.
   ●Any loss of balance or unsafe behavior occurs during the task.
10. Precautions
(1) Participant Precautions
   ●A basic warm-up shall be completed before capture, especially for the shoulders, elbows, wrists, neck, and upper back.
   ●Natural breathing shall be maintained throughout the motion; breath-holding should be avoided.
   ●Participants shall not deliberately exaggerate arm amplitude, wrist movement, or trunk motion merely to match the protocol.
   ●If any discomfort occurs, especially in the shoulder, elbow, wrist, neck, or upper back, the participant shall immediately notify the researcher and stop the experiment.
(2) Researcher Precautions
   ●The motion standard shall be explained uniformly before capture, and demonstration may be provided if necessary.
   ●The safety of the capture area and the stability of all equipment shall be confirmed before each trial.
   ●All participants should complete the task under the same or similar conditions.
   ●Any abnormal event during capture, such as sensor looseness, excessive shoulder elevation, unstable posture, or motion deviation, shall be recorded.
11. Termination Criteria
The capture shall be terminated immediately if any of the following occurs:
   ●The participant reports pain, dizziness, fatigue, or other obvious physical discomfort.
   ●The participant shows clear balance instability or inability to continue the task safely.
   ●Equipment failure or sensor detachment prevents valid data collection.
   ●The researcher determines that continuing the trial may pose a safety risk.
12. Data Quality Control Recommendations
   ●One trial capture is recommended before formal recording to verify motion compliance, environmental safety, and equipment stability.
   ●Capture duration, elbow flexion range, shoulder flexion range, upper-arm position, wrist motion characteristics, and abnormal events shall be documented for each trial.
   ●Data segments that clearly deviate from protocol requirements should be labeled and, if necessary, excluded or re-collected.
