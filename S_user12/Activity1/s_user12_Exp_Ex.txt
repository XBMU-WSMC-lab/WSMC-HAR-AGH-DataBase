Jogging Motion Capture Protocol
1. Motion Name
Jogging
2. Objective
This protocol is intended to standardize the jogging behavior of participants during motion capture in order to ensure consistency, repeatability, and reliability of the collected data. 
This motion is primarily used to record kinematic characteristics and related physiological signals during light- to moderate-intensity running.
3. Applicable Subjects
This protocol is applicable to participants who have basic independent mobility and are able to safely perform light- to moderate-intensity jogging. Individuals with acute musculoskeletal 
injuries, significant fatigue, cardiovascular discomfort, or any other condition unsuitable for physical activity should not participate in this motion capture task.
4. Environment and Equipment Requirements
(1)The capture area shall be flat, non-slip, unobstructed, and provide sufficient safe space for movement.
(2)If a treadmill is used, the speed settings shall be calibrated in advance and the device shall operate stably throughout the session.
(3)If wearable sensors are used, they shall be positioned correctly, securely attached, and free from noticeable looseness or displacement.
(4)Participants shall wear appropriate sportswear and athletic shoes. Loose clothing that may interfere with movement or obstruct equipment should be avoided.
5.Motion Definition
The participant performs jogging in a natural, relaxed, and continuous manner while maintaining a stable rhythm and coordinated gait. During the motion, moderate arm swing, gentle foot contact,
and a slight forward lean of the trunk shall be maintained. Obvious tension, exaggerated movement, sudden acceleration or deceleration, and distorted movement patterns should be avoided.
6.Motion Execution Requirements
(1)Starting Posture
   ●The participant stands at the designated starting position in a natural upright posture.
   ●The head remains stable, with the gaze directed forward.
   ●The shoulders remain relaxed, and the trunk is kept in a neutral position.
   ●The arms are naturally bent at the elbows, and the hands remain relaxed.
   ●The feet are placed naturally, approximately hip-width apart.
(2)Motion Procedure
   ●Upon instruction, the participant begins jogging and reaches a stable rhythm as soon as possible.
   ●During jogging, the participant shall maintain a natural and relaxed body state without deliberately exaggerating the movement.
   ●The arms shall swing forward and backward with moderate amplitude, primarily in the sagittal plane, while excessive lateral arm movement should be avoided.
   ●The trunk shall maintain a slight forward lean of approximately 5°–10° relative to the vertical axis. This forward lean shall come from the whole-body posture rather than from bending at the waist or hunching the shoulders.
   ●Foot contact shall be gentle and smooth. A mid-/forefoot strike or a natural full-foot landing is recommended.
   ●Step length shall remain moderate and should be neither excessively large nor excessively small.
   ●Running speed shall remain stable throughout the capture period, with no unnecessary acceleration, deceleration, pausing, or switching to a different movement pattern.
(3)Quantitative Control Indicators
   ●Speed: 6–8 km/h
   ●Cadence: 150–165 steps/min
   ●Forward trunk lean: 5°–10° relative to the vertical axis
   ●Arm swing: natural, symmetrical, and moderate in amplitude
   ●Foot strike: gentle and stable, preferably mid-/forefoot strike or natural full-foot contact
7.Capture Procedure
(1)Preparation Phase
   ●Explain the motion requirements and precautions to the participant.
   ●Check the operating status of all equipment and the placement of wearable sensors.
   ●Ensure that the participant completes the necessary warm-up.
(2)Preliminary Phase
   ●The participant first performs a short trial jog at a comfortable rhythm.
   ●Adjust the motion until the target speed and cadence range are reached.
   ●Begin formal capture only after the movement pattern is stable.
(3)Main Experiment Phase
   ●The researcher gives the start instruction.
   ●The participant performs continuous jogging in accordance with this protocol.
   ●During the capture, the researcher monitors whether the motion conforms to the required standard.
(4)Termination Phase
   ●At the end of the scheduled capture duration, the researcher gives the stop instruction.
   ●The participant gradually slows down and comes to a stop.
   ●The researcher checks data quality and records any abnormal events or deviations.
8.Criteria for a Valid Trial
A jogging trial may be considered valid if all of the following conditions are met:
   ●The running posture is natural and relaxed, with no obvious stiffness or tension.   
   ●Speed is maintained within the range of 6–8 km/h.   
   ●Cadence is maintained within the range of 150–165 steps/min.   
   ●Forward trunk lean generally remains within 5°–10°.   
   ●Arm swing is coordinated, symmetrical, and moderate in amplitude.   
   ●Foot contact is gentle, with no obvious stomping or heavy impact on the ground.   
   ●The movement remains continuous throughout the trial, with no clear pauses, distortions, or extraneous actions.
9.Common Incorrect Patterns
The following conditions shall be regarded as motion deviations and may require re-capture:
   ●Excessive forward lean, backward lean, or obvious bending at the waist.
   ●A lowered head or frequent left-right head movement that affects body stability.
   ●Arm swing that is excessively large, excessively small, or asymmetrical.
   ●Heavy foot strike or obvious stomping.
   ●Overstriding that makes the motion unnatural, or clearly unstable cadence.
   ●Sudden acceleration, deceleration, stopping, or turning during capture.
   ●Excessive side-to-side body sway or uncoordinated running posture.
10.Precautions
(1)Participant Precautions
   ●A basic warm-up shall be completed before capture, with particular attention to the ankles, knees, and hips.
   ●Natural breathing shall be maintained throughout the motion; breath-holding should be avoided.
   ●Participants shall not adopt an obviously artificial running style merely to match the protocol.
   ●If any discomfort occurs, the participant shall immediately notify the researcher and stop the experiment.
(2)Researcher Precautions
   ●The motion standard shall be explained uniformly before capture, and demonstration may be provided if necessary.
   ●If a treadmill is used, sufficient adaptation time shall be provided.
   ●All participants should complete the task under the same or similar conditions.
   ●Any abnormal event during capture, such as sensor looseness, unstable cadence, or motion deviation, shall be recorded.
11.Termination Criteria
The capture shall be terminated immediately if any of the following occurs:
   ●The participant reports dizziness, chest discomfort, shortness of breath, pain, or other obvious physical discomfort.
   ●The participant shows clear gait instability or risk of falling.
   ●Equipment failure or sensor detachment prevents valid data collection.
   ●The researcher determines that continuing the trial may pose a safety risk.
12.Data Quality Control Recommendations
   ●One trial capture is recommended before formal recording to verify motion compliance and equipment stability.
   ●Speed, cadence, capture duration, and abnormal events shall be documented for each trial.
   ●Data segments that clearly deviate from protocol requirements should be labeled and, if necessary, excluded or re-collected.
