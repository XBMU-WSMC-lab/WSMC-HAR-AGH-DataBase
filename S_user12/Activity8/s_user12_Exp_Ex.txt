Squatting Motion Capture Protocol
1. Motion Name
Squatting
2. Objective
This protocol is intended to standardize the squatting behavior of participants during motion capture in order to ensure consistency, repeatability, and reliability of the collected data.
This motion is primarily used to record lower-limb and trunk kinematic characteristics and related physiological signals during controlled squat movement.
3. Applicable Subjects
This protocol is applicable to participants who have basic independent standing and squatting ability and are able to safely perform repeated squat movements.
Individuals with acute musculoskeletal injuries, severe pain in the hip, knee, ankle, or lower back, significant fatigue, balance impairment, cardiovascular discomfort, or any other condition unsuitable for squatting should not participate in this motion capture task.
4. Environment and Equipment Requirements
(1) The capture area shall be flat, stable, non-slip, unobstructed, and provide sufficient space for safe squat execution.
(2) If wearable sensors or markers are used, they shall be positioned correctly, securely attached, and free from noticeable looseness or displacement.
(3) Participants shall wear appropriate clothing and footwear that do not restrict lower-limb movement or obscure markers.
(4) The participant shall stand in a clearly defined capture area with enough frontal and lateral clearance to complete the movement naturally.
(5) If necessary, protective supervision shall be provided during the task to reduce safety risks.
5. Motion Definition
The participant performs a squat by first standing upright with the feet positioned laterally congruent with the shoulders and the forefeet everted approximately 5–5 degrees. 
A neutral spinal posture shall be maintained throughout the movement. The body is lowered until the thighs are nearly parallel to the ground, with the knees bent to approximately 90 degrees. 
The participant then presses through the heels to return to a standing position with fully extended knees, while avoiding excessive knee extension. The overall motion shall remain natural, controlled, stable, and continuous. 
Excessive forward trunk flexion, heel lifting, knee collapse, unstable foot placement, or abrupt movement should be avoided.
6. Motion Execution Requirements
(1) Starting Posture
   ●The participant stands at the designated starting position in a natural upright posture.
   ●The head remains stable, with the gaze directed forward.
   ●The shoulders remain relaxed, and the trunk is kept in a neutral position.
   ●The spine shall remain in a neutral posture.
   ●The feet are placed approximately shoulder-width apart.
   ●The forefeet are everted approximately 5–5 degrees.
   ●The arms may be placed naturally according to the experimental design, provided they do not interfere with data acquisition.
(2) Motion Procedure
   ●Upon instruction, the participant begins the squat movement in a controlled manner.
   ●The participant lowers the body by flexing the hips and knees while maintaining a neutral spinal posture.
   ●The feet shall remain stable on the ground throughout the movement.
   ●The body shall be lowered until the thighs are nearly parallel to the ground.
   ●The knees shall be bent to approximately 90 degrees at the lowest position.
   ●The participant then presses through the heels to return to the standing position.
   ●At the end of the ascent, the knees shall return to full extension without excessive hyperextension.
   ●The movement shall remain natural, stable, and continuous throughout the capture period.
(3) Quantitative Control Indicators
   ●Body posture: upright standing at start and end
   ●Foot position: approximately shoulder-width apart
   ●Forefoot angle: 5–5 degrees of eversion
   ●Spinal posture: neutral
   ●Squat depth: thighs nearly parallel to the ground
   ●Knee angle at lowest position: approximately 90 degrees
   ●Return phase: heel-driven ascent to standing with full knee extension and no excessive hyperextension
   ●Movement quality: natural, controlled, stable, and continuous
7. Capture Procedure
(1) Preparation Phase
   ●Explain the motion requirements and precautions to the participant.
   ●Check the operating status of all equipment and the placement of wearable sensors or markers.
   ●Inspect the capture area and surrounding environment for safety.
   ●Ensure that the participant completes the necessary warm-up, especially for the hips, knees, ankles, and lower back.
(2) Preliminary Phase
   ●The participant first performs a short trial of squatting at a comfortable pace.
   ●Adjust the motion until the required foot position, spinal posture, squat depth, and return pattern are achieved.
   ●Begin formal capture only after the movement pattern is stable.
(3) Main Experiment Phase
   ●The researcher gives the start instruction.
   ●The participant performs squatting in accordance with this protocol.
   ●During the capture, the researcher monitors whether the motion conforms to the required standard.
   ●If necessary, the researcher provides immediate reminder or safety supervision.
(4) Termination Phase
   ●At the end of the scheduled capture, the researcher gives the stop instruction.
   ●The participant returns to a stable natural standing posture.
   ●The researcher checks data quality and records any abnormal events or deviations.
8. Criteria for a Valid Trial
A squatting trial may be considered valid if all of the following conditions are met:
   ●The participant maintains a stable upright posture at the start and end of the movement.
   ●The feet remain approximately shoulder-width apart with forefoot eversion of about 5–5 degrees.
   ●A neutral spinal posture is maintained throughout the movement.
   ●The participant lowers the body until the thighs are nearly parallel to the ground.
   ●The knees are bent to approximately 90 degrees at the lowest position.
   ●The participant returns to standing by pressing through the heels.
   ●The knees reach full extension at the end without obvious hyperextension.
   ●The movement is natural, controlled, continuous, and free of obvious instability or compensation.
9. Common Incorrect Patterns
The following conditions shall be regarded as motion deviations and may require re-capture:
   ●The feet are placed too narrow or too wide relative to shoulder width.
   ●The forefeet are not within the approximate 5–5 degree eversion range.
   ●The spine is not maintained in a neutral posture, with obvious excessive trunk flexion or extension.
   ●The squat depth is insufficient, and the thighs do not approach parallel to the ground.
   ●The knees are markedly less or more flexed than approximately 90 degrees at the lowest position.
   ●The heels lift off the ground during descent or ascent.
   ●The knees collapse inward or move in an unstable manner.
   ●The participant returns to standing without clear heel-driven force or with obvious knee hyperextension.
   ●The movement rhythm is unstable, with hesitation, abrupt motion, or poor control.
   ●Any loss of balance or unsafe behavior occurs during the task.
10. Precautions
(1) Participant Precautions
   ●A basic warm-up shall be completed before capture, with particular attention to the hips, knees, ankles, and lower back.
   ●Natural breathing shall be maintained throughout the motion; breath-holding should be avoided.
   ●Participants shall not deliberately exaggerate squat depth, trunk motion, or ascent force merely to match the protocol.
   ●If any discomfort occurs, especially in the hip, knee, ankle, or lower back, the participant shall immediately notify the researcher and stop the experiment.
(2) Researcher Precautions
   ●The motion standard shall be explained uniformly before capture, and demonstration may be provided if necessary.
   ●The safety of the capture area and the stability of all equipment shall be confirmed before each trial.
   ●All participants should complete the task under the same or similar conditions.
   ●Any abnormal event during capture, such as sensor looseness, posture instability, insufficient depth, or motion deviation, shall be recorded.
11. Termination Criteria
The capture shall be terminated immediately if any of the following occurs:
   ●The participant reports pain, dizziness, fatigue, or other obvious physical discomfort.
   ●The participant shows clear balance instability or inability to continue the task safely.
   ●Equipment failure or sensor detachment prevents valid data collection.
   ●The researcher determines that continuing the trial may pose a safety risk.
12. Data Quality Control Recommendations
   ●One trial capture is recommended before formal recording to verify motion compliance, environmental safety, and equipment stability.
   ●Capture duration, foot position, forefoot angle, squat depth, knee angle, and abnormal events shall be documented for each trial.
   ●Data segments that clearly deviate from protocol requirements should be labeled and, if necessary, excluded or re-collected.
