Running Motion Capture Protocol
1. Motion Name
Running
2. Objective
This protocol standardizes the running performance of subjects during data collection to ensure biomechanical consistency and reproducibility. The objective is to capture kinematic 
characteristics of moderate-intensity running, with a specific focus on stride length, arm swing amplitude, and hip extension.
3. Applicable Subjects
TSubjects with adequate physical fitness who can safely perform moderate-intensity running tasks. Individuals with acute injuries, cardiopulmonary limitations, 
or recent history of excessive fatigue are excluded.
4. Environment and Equipment Requirements
(1)The capture area must be spacious and flat, providing sufficient room for acceleration and deceleration.
(2)For treadmill-based capture, speed must be calibrated beforehand, and a safety tether is required to prevent falls.
(3)Sensors should be secured tightly against the skin or compression wear using medical tape or straps to prevent data artifacts caused by high-frequency vibration.
5.Motion Definition
The subject performs running in a coordinated, continuous gait. Compared to jogging, running requires increased stride length and more active arm swings. 
During the propulsion phase, full hip extension must be maintained. The trunk should remain neutral or slightly tilted forward; backward leaning is strictly prohibited.
6.Motion Execution Requirements
(1)Start and Transition
   ●Starting from a standing position, the subject gradually accelerates to the target speed range.
   ●Data recording begins once the cadence and gait pattern have stabilized.
(2)Key Technical Points
   ●Stride & Cadence: Increase stride length to maintain an extended gait, with cadence strictly controlled between 165–180 steps/min.
   ●Arm Swing: Arm swing amplitude should be larger than that in jogging, moving primarily in the sagittal plane to drive the rhythmic movement.
   ●Hip Extension: Emphasize full hip extension during the push-off phase to provide effective propulsion.
   ●Trunk Posture: Avoid backward leaning; maintain core stability with a slight, natural forward lean.
   ●Foot Strike: A midfoot or forefoot strike is recommended to ensure a smooth transition and minimize impact forces.
(3)Quantitative Control Indicators
   ●Speed: 10–13 km/h
   ●Cadence: 165–180 steps/min
   ●Forward trunk lean: 5°–10° relative to the vertical axis
   ●Posture: Maintain hip extension; no backward leaning
   ●Arm Swing: Significantly larger than jogging
7.Capture Procedure
(1)Preparation Phase
   ●Explain the motion requirements and precautions to the participant.
   ●Check the operating status of all equipment and the placement of wearable sensors.
   ●Ensure that the participant completes the necessary warm-up.
(2)Preliminary Phase
   ●The participant first performs a short trial jog at a comfortable rhythm.
   ●Adjust the motion until the target speed and cadence range are reached.
   ●Begin formal capture only after the movement pattern is stable.
(3)Main Experiment Phase
   ●The researcher gives the start instruction.
   ●The participant performs continuous running in accordance with this protocol.
   ●During the capture, the researcher monitors whether the motion conforms to the required standard.
(4)Termination Phase
   ●At the end of the scheduled capture duration, the researcher gives the stop instruction.
   ●The participant gradually slows down and comes to a stop.
   ●The researcher checks data quality and records any abnormal events or deviations.
8.Criteria for a Valid Trial
A running trial may be considered valid if all of the following conditions are met:
   ●The running posture is natural and relaxed, with no obvious stiffness or tension.   
   ●Speed is maintained within the range of 10–13 km/h.   
   ●Cadence is maintained within the range of 165–180 steps/min.   
   ●Stride length is clearly greater than that of jogging and remains natural.   
   ●Arm swing is coordinated, symmetrical, and greater than that of jogging.   
   ●Hip extension is maintained, and no obvious backward leaning is observed.
   ●Foot contact is smooth, with no obvious stomping or heavy impact on the ground.   
   ●The movement remains continuous throughout the trial, with no clear pauses, distortions, or extraneous actions.
9.Common Incorrect Patterns
The following conditions shall be regarded as motion deviations and may require re-capture:
   ●Backward leaning of the trunk during running.
   ●Insufficient hip extension, resulting in limited propulsion or shortened running posture.
   ●Arm swing that is excessively small, excessively large, or asymmetrical.
   ●Stride length that is too short to reflect natural running, or excessively large to the point of overstriding.
   ●Heavy foot strike or obvious stomping.
   ●Sudden acceleration, deceleration, stopping, or turning during capture.
   ●Excessive side-to-side body sway or uncoordinated running posture.
   ●A lowered head or frequent left-right head movement that affects body stability.  
10.Precautions
(1)Participant Precautions
   ●A basic warm-up shall be completed before capture, with particular attention to the ankles, knees, and hips.
   ●Natural breathing shall be maintained throughout the motion; breath-holding should be avoided.
   ●Participants shall not adopt an obviously artificial running style merely to match the protocol.
   ●If any discomfort occurs, the participant shall immediately notify the researcher and stop the experiment.
(2)Researcher Precautions
   ●The motion standard shall be explained uniformly before capture, and demonstration may be provided if necessary.
   ●If a treadmill is used, sufficient adaptation time shall be provided.
   ●All participants should complete the task under the same or similar conditions.
   ●Any abnormal event during capture, such as sensor looseness, unstable cadence, or motion deviation, shall be recorded.
11.Termination Criteria
The capture shall be terminated immediately if any of the following occurs:
   ●The participant reports dizziness, chest discomfort, shortness of breath, pain, or other obvious physical discomfort.
   ●The participant shows clear gait instability or risk of falling.
   ●Equipment failure or sensor detachment prevents valid data collection.
   ●The researcher determines that continuing the trial may pose a safety risk.
12.Data Quality Control Recommendations
   ●One trial capture is recommended before formal recording to verify motion compliance and equipment stability.
   ●Speed, cadence, capture duration, and abnormal events shall be documented for each trial.
   ●Data segments that clearly deviate from protocol requirements should be labeled and, if necessary, excluded or re-collected.
