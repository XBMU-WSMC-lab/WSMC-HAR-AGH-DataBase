Clapping Motion Capture Protocol
1. Motion Name
Clapping
2. Objective
This protocol is intended to standardize the clapping behavior of participants during motion capture in order to ensure consistency, repeatability, and reliability of the collected data.
This motion is primarily used to record upper-limb kinematic characteristics and related physiological signals during rhythmic bilateral hand movement.
3. Applicable Subjects
This protocol is applicable to participants who have basic independent standing ability and are able to safely perform repeated bilateral upper-limb movements.
Individuals with acute upper-limb musculoskeletal injuries, severe shoulder or elbow pain, significant fatigue, balance impairment, cardiovascular discomfort, or any other condition unsuitable for physical activity should not participate in this motion capture task.
4. Environment and Equipment Requirements
(1) The capture area shall be flat, stable, non-slip, unobstructed, and provide sufficient safe space for upper-limb movement.
(2) If wearable sensors or markers are used, they shall be positioned correctly, securely attached, and free from noticeable looseness or displacement.
(3) Participants shall wear appropriate clothing that does not restrict upper-limb motion or obscure markers.
(4) The participant shall stand in a clearly defined capture area with enough frontal and lateral clearance to complete the motion naturally.
(5) If the participant has mild balance concerns, protective supervision shall be provided during the task.
5. Motion Definition
The participant performs clapping while maintaining an upright standing posture. During the motion, both hands are rapidly brought into contact and then separated at chest height in a rhythmic and coordinated manner. 
The elbows remain slightly bent, and the inter-hand distance during separation shall generally be maintained within the range of 10–0 cm. 
The movement shall remain natural, continuous, and symmetrical as far as possible. Obvious trunk compensation, exaggerated arm swinging, sudden stopping, or distorted movement patterns should be avoided.
6. Motion Execution Requirements
(1) Starting Posture
   ●The participant stands at the designated starting position in a natural upright posture.
   ●The head remains stable, with the gaze directed forward.
   ●The shoulders remain relaxed, and the trunk is kept in a neutral position.
   ●Both upper arms are naturally positioned, with the elbows slightly bent.
   ●Both hands are positioned in front of the chest in preparation for clapping.
   ●The feet are placed naturally, approximately hip-width apart.
(2) Motion Procedure
   ●Upon instruction, the participant begins clapping at chest height.
   ●Both hands shall move toward each other rapidly and make contact in front of the chest.
   ●After contact, both hands shall separate in a controlled manner while maintaining the motion rhythm.
   ●The elbows shall remain slightly bent throughout the movement.
   ●The inter-hand distance during separation shall generally be maintained within the range of 10–0 cm.
   ●The hands shall remain approximately at chest height during repeated clapping.
   ●The trunk shall remain stable, and excessive forward leaning, backward leaning, rotation, or side-to-side sway should be avoided.
   ●The movement shall remain natural, rhythmic, and continuous throughout the capture period.
(3) Quantitative Control Indicators
   ●Body posture: upright standing
   ●Hand movement: rapid contact and separation
   ●Hand position: chest height
   ●Elbow angle: slightly bent
   ●Inter-hand distance during separation: 10–0 cm
   ●Movement quality: natural, rhythmic, stable, and continuous
7. Capture Procedure
(1) Preparation Phase
   ●Explain the motion requirements and precautions to the participant.
   ●Check the operating status of all equipment and the placement of wearable sensors or markers.
   ●Inspect the capture area and surrounding environment for safety.
   ●Ensure that the participant completes the necessary warm-up, especially for the shoulders, elbows, wrists, and hands.
(2) Preliminary Phase
   ●The participant first performs a short trial of clapping at a comfortable rhythm.
   ●Adjust the motion until the required hand position, rhythm, and movement pattern are reached.
   ●Begin formal capture only after the movement pattern is stable.
(3) Main Experiment Phase
   ●The researcher gives the start instruction.
   ●The participant performs clapping in accordance with this protocol.
   ●During the capture, the researcher monitors whether the motion conforms to the required standard.
   ●If necessary, the researcher provides immediate reminder or safety supervision.
(4) Termination Phase
   ●At the end of the scheduled capture, the researcher gives the stop instruction.
   ●The participant returns to a stable natural standing posture.
   ●The researcher checks data quality and records any abnormal events or deviations.
8. Criteria for a Valid Trial
A clapping trial may be considered valid if all of the following conditions are met:
   ●The participant maintains an upright and stable standing posture throughout the trial.
   ●Both hands perform repeated contact and separation at approximately chest height.
   ●The elbows remain slightly bent throughout the movement.
   ●The inter-hand distance during separation is generally maintained within 10–0 cm.
   ●The movement is rhythmic, continuous, and coordinated, without obvious interruption or distortion.
   ●No obvious trunk compensation, loss of balance, or unsafe event occurs during the task.
9. Common Incorrect Patterns
The following conditions shall be regarded as motion deviations and may require re-capture:
   ●Hand contact occurs clearly above or below the required chest-height region.
   ●The inter-hand distance during separation is clearly smaller than 10 cm or greater than 40 cm for a sustained period.
   ●The elbows are excessively extended or excessively flexed, deviating from the slightly bent position.
   ●The participant uses excessive trunk movement, shoulder elevation, or exaggerated arm swinging.
   ●The movement rhythm is unstable, with obvious hesitation, pausing, or abrupt changes in speed.
   ●The clapping motion is asymmetrical or poorly coordinated.
   ●Any loss of balance or unsafe behavior occurs during the task.
10. Precautions
(1) Participant Precautions
   ●A basic warm-up shall be completed before capture, with particular attention to the shoulders, elbows, wrists, and fingers.
   ●Natural breathing shall be maintained throughout the motion; breath-holding should be avoided.
   ●Participants shall not adopt an obviously artificial or exaggerated clapping style merely to match the protocol.
   ●If any discomfort occurs, especially in the shoulder, elbow, or wrist, the participant shall immediately notify the researcher and stop the experiment.
(2) Researcher Precautions
   ●The motion standard shall be explained uniformly before capture, and demonstration may be provided if necessary.
   ●The safety of the capture area and the stability of all equipment shall be confirmed before each trial.
   ●All participants should complete the task under the same or similar conditions.
   ●Any abnormal event during capture, such as sensor looseness, unstable rhythm, or motion deviation, shall be recorded.
11. Termination Criteria
The capture shall be terminated immediately if any of the following occurs:
   ●The participant reports pain, dizziness, fatigue, or other obvious physical discomfort.
   ●The participant shows clear balance instability or inability to continue the task safely.
   ●Equipment failure or sensor detachment prevents valid data collection.
   ●The researcher determines that continuing the trial may pose a safety risk.
12. Data Quality Control Recommendations
   ●One trial capture is recommended before formal recording to verify motion compliance, environmental safety, and equipment stability.
   ●Capture duration, hand position, movement rhythm, inter-hand distance, and abnormal events shall be documented for each trial.
   ●Data segments that clearly deviate from protocol requirements should be labeled and, if necessary, excluded or re-collected.
